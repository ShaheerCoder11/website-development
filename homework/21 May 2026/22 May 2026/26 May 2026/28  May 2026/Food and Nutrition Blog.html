    • How to Build a Healthy Palate

    Why a balance meal matters

    A "balanced plate" is a visual tool used to build healthy, nutritious meals

    Tips fpr balance Palate

    • fill ½ of your plate with vegetables, ¼ with lean protein, and ¼ with complex carbohydrates, topped with a drizzle of healthy fats
    • ½ Non-Starchy Vegetables: Load up on colorful vegetables like spinach, broccoli, bell peppers, or carrot

    Food best for better palates

    1. Fresh Herbs and Spices: Replace heavy, artificial dipping sauces with natural enhancers like garlic, cilantro, rosemary, paprika, or turmeric to appreciate the natural flavor profiles of your meals
    2. Bitter Greens: Leafy greens (arugula, spinach, and kale) introduce bitterness to your palate while encouraging a healthy oral microbiome
    3. Cleanse Between Bites: Keep your palate sharp by sipping sparkling water, chewing on a sprig of parsley, or eating a thin slice of apple or pickled ginger between different dishes.

    Drink enough food

    Drinking enough water is essential to maintain energy levels, regulate body temperature, and prevent dehydration. Most guidelines recommend consuming 2 to 3.7 liters of fluid daily depending on your size, gender, and activity level. A simple rule is to drink enough so that your urine remains a pale, clear yellow


    Eat Colorful Food

    "Eating the rainbow" is an evidence-backed method to ensure your body gets a full spectrum of essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants.
